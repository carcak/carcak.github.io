Research Experience

(2016 - 2022) Geospatial Analyst

Charles Darwin Foundation (CDF),
Galápagos Islands, Ecuador.

Job Description: Map invasive, native and endemic plant species in the Galápagos Islands to guide conservation actions carried out by the Galápagos National Park Directorate (GNPD).
Methodology: Mapping was attained with machine learning algorithms applied to very high resolution multispectral satellite imagery. Ground data was collected using photogrammetrically processed drone imagery.

Activities:
  • Contribute to the writing of research proposals and results to local authorities and donors.
  • Present research results to scientific community in conferences and symposia.
  • Participate in community outreach strategies with written and audiovisual material, talks, workshops and other educational activities.
  • Select, train and supervise project volunteers and interns on project specific tasks.
  • Lead expeditions and pilot drone flights in the protected area of the Galápagos Islands.

Forstliche Versuchs- und Forschungsanstalt Baden Württemberg (Forest Research Institute),
Freiburg, Germany.

Conception and development of models for various wood harvesting scenarios and database management, in a project to assess soil nutrient extraction due to wood harvesting.
